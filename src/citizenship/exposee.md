## Professionalism

Conduct or ways which marks a professinal accross his field or domain.

Professionalism is perceived via a set of attributes which make of one profession,
proffesionalism and a person which executes such attributes is a professional,
and include:

- **Expertise or specialized knowledge**: A professional has expertise in his
  domain, and knows what he or she is doing. A professional has acquired skills,
  degrees and certificates. e.g a PHD in law means one has specialized knowledge
  in law.
- **Competency**: The get the job done and don't make excuses and focus solely
  on finding solutions, this is actually tight to their **Expertise**.
- **Honesty and Integrity**: ...
- **Accountability**: A professional should be able to open up and be accountable
  if he makes mistakes while doing his job, he should be fully responsible of
  his mistakes.
- **Regulation**: He should be emotionaly intelligent and regulate properly
  his interactions with others:
  - Comming early.
  - Be polite.
  - Punctuality.
  - *Self-discipline*.
- **Up to date**: A professional keeps himself up to date.
- **Active listening and communication**: He should be able to convey his
  message, using the right words, not listening to everything but what is
  important.
- **Have the right tools**: He should have the right tools to use and inform
  himself.
- **Time management**: A profesional should have defined time frames for
  his activities.


## Good governance

Governance is the decision and implementation.

Decisions are made in consideration of every affected person(what citizens wish
and need, ...) and implemented fairly.

> Good governance is the transparent, accountable and efficient management of a
> government, organization or other institution's resources and members.

### Principles of good governance

- **Parcitipation**: Everyone should participate, directly or inderectly.
  - **Direct participation**:
    - A minister or president directly governs.
  - **Indirect**
    - A teacher indirectly help students
- **Transparency**: The institution's governance should be
  completely transparent and well-known by citizens.
- **Responsiveness**: It should try to meet up to all stakeholders needs
  (farmers, citizens, ...).

- **Accountability**: The institution should be accountable to it's citizens
  of it's actions and how it uses resources.
- **Participation**: Citizens should participate in decision-making.
- **Rules of law**: The law should apply to every citizen or administration
  member, _no one is above the law_ .
- **Human rights**: Humans rights are part of the law and should always be
  respected.
- **Good decision making and implementation**: They should make good decisions
  and implement those decisions fairly.
